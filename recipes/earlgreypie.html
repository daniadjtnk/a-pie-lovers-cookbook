EARL GREY PIE

WHY YOU SHOULD COOK THIS RECIPE

Earl Grey Pie is a delightful dessert that combines the subtle citrusy flavor of Earl Grey tea with the creaminess of a classic pie.
This dessert, with its velvety smooth texture and aromatic tea-infused custard, offers a unique and sophisticated twist to traditional pies.

INGREDIENT

  • Pie Crust
    • 1 ½ cups of crushed graham crackers
    • ⅓ cup of melted butter
    • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • Earl Grey Filling
    • 1 ½ cups of milk
    • ½ cup of heavy cream
    • 3 tablespoons of Earl Grey loose tea leaves
    • ⅓ cup of sugar
    • 3 egg yolks
    • ¼ cup of cornstarch
    • A pinch of salt

HOW TO MAKE EARL GREY PIE

  1. Prepare the Crust:
    • Combine the crushed graham crackers, melted butter, and sugar in a bowl. Press the mixture into a pie dish and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 8-10 minutes until lightly golden. Let it cool.
  2. Infuse the Tea:
    • In a saucepan, heat the milk and heavy cream until just simmering. Remove from heat, add Earl Grey tea leaves, cover, and let it steep for 15-20 minutes. Strain the mixture to remove the tea leaves.
  3. Make the Filling:
    • In a clean saucepan, whisk together sugar, egg yolks, cornstarch, and a pinch of salt. Gradually pour in the Earl Grey-infused milk and cream mixture, whisking continuously.
    • Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly until it thickens to a custard-like consistency.
  4. Assemble the Pie:
    • Pour the Earl Grey custard into the cooled pie crust. Smooth the top with a spatula.
    • Refrigerate the pie for at least 4 hours or until set.
  5. Serve:
    • Slice the Earl Grey Pie and serve chilled. Optionally, garnish with whipped cream or fresh berries.

This Earl Grey Pie recipe offers a delightful harmony of flavors and textures, making it an elegant dessert choice for any occasion.
